Risk Spectrum	HIGH RISK	MEDIUM RISK	LOW RISK
The Rural Poor lack access to electricity and live in extreme poverty. Many are likely to engage in subsistence farming but lack access to an intact cold chain that would enable them to sell their products further afield at a higher price. There may also be a lack of medical cold chains in rural poor communities, putting lives at risk from spoiled medicines and vaccines.

The Urban Poor have limited or no access to electricity, and whose quality of housing is likely too poor to protect them from extreme heat. Their income may not be sufficient to purchase or run a fan. They may own or have access to a refrigerator, but intermittent electricity supplies may mean that food often spoils and there is a high risk of poor nutrition or food poisoning.	The Lower-Middle Income represent an increasingly affluent lower-middle income class that is on the brink of purchasing the lowest first-cost air conditioner or refrigerator on the market. Limited purchasing choices available to this group favour cooling devices that are likely affordable, but inefficient.	The Middle Income typically own an air conditioner and a refrigerator and may be able to afford more efficient ones. They may also be able to move to better-designed, more efficient housing and working environments, where they might also make conscious choices to minimize the use of or not own an air-conditioning unit.

Findings and Trends	Urban vs. Rural Risk: Urban populations face growing high-risk cooling vulnerability, while rural areas experience mixed trajectories in risk, depending on the region and country. Slow progress – or even backsliding - in electricity access exacerbates cooling vulnerability.

Medium-Risk Group Growth: 2 billion people in the medium-risk group face a "double burden" of unaffordable or inefficient cooling options. This group is at risk of locking into ineficcient, but also high-emissions solutions, unless efficient, sustainable cooling is prioritized.

Polarization by 2030: Projections show a rise in both high- and low-risk populations while medium-risk groups decline, creating growing inequality in cooling access. Countries like Brazil, Bangladesh, and Indonesia face widening disparities, requiring targeted interventions.

Sub-Saharan Africa’s Persistent Vulnerability: Sub-Saharan Africa has the largest high-risk population, with limited growth in low-risk groups. Despite some medium-risk expansion, infrastructure gaps, poverty and pressure on urban systems continue to hinder progress, demanding urgent solutions.
