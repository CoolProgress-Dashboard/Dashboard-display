Every efficiency standard set or skipped this decade, every refrigerant phase-down met or delayed, every billion people left without cooling: these decisions are being locked in right now. The window to act at scale closes before 2030. What's been missing is a single place where the evidence is visible, comparable, and ready for the room where the decision is made.

CoolProgress is that place.

The Framework

Five questions. Five pillars. Now we'll show them live.

We organised around the questions, not the data.

  • 01
    How big is the cooling sector's emissions footprint?

    ~7% of global GHGs, split 70% indirect (electricity) and 30% direct (refrigerant leaks). Two streams, two policy levers.

    Emissions
  • 02
    Are efficiency standards rising fast enough?

    The best AC sold today is 3× more efficient than the worst still legal. Harmonised MEPS globally would save ~1,300 TWh per year.

    Product Efficiency
  • 03
    Is the HFC phase-down on track?

    172 countries ratified Kigali. Full compliance prevents ~0.5°C of warming. The dashboard tracks whether ratification turned into follow-through.

    Refrigerant Transition
  • 04
    Who is being left behind on cooling access?

    1.2 billion people face dangerous heat without adequate cooling. Cold chains, heat stress and food security mapped country by country.

    Access & Vulnerability
  • 05
    What is the state of pledges and national commitments?

    74 countries and 235 cities signed the Global Cooling Pledge at COP28. NDC mentions and National Cooling Action Plans tracked in real time.

    National Plans & Commitments

Emissions

70 % of cooling emissions are electricity. 30 % are refrigerant leaks. Two problems, two policy levers.

02
Product Efficiency

The best AC is 3× more efficient than the worst still legally sold.

Global MEPS harmonisation = ~1,300 TWh/yr saved. India went 31% to 82% inverter penetration in 5 years.

▶ /dashboard/meps
03
Refrigerant Transition

172 countries ratified Kigali. Following through is the harder half.

Full compliance prevents ~0.5°C of warming. The refrigerant bank is still growing: 5–6 Mt circulating today.

▶ /dashboard/kigali
04
Access & Vulnerability

1.2 billion people face dangerous heat without adequate cooling, mapped country by country.

420,000 deaths/yr from broken cold chains. 40% post-harvest food loss in Sub-Saharan Africa.

▶ /dashboard/access
05
National Plans & Commitments

74 countries + 235 cities signed the Global Cooling Pledge. NDC and NCAP coverage is the gap you can close.

The largest political commitment ever made on cooling, signed at COP28. Phase 2 tracks follow-through.
