Understanding the Problem

The Climate Crisis

Cooling is driving the planet's temperature up.

A fast-growing, largely untracked emission source. A single refrigerant leak can release gases more than 2,000× more potent than CO₂.

7% of global GHG emissions from cooling today
×3 demand projected to triple by 2050
0.5°C extra warming from HFCs alone if unchecked
The Human Crisis

And it's leaving the most vulnerable behind.

For billions in low- and middle-income countries, cooling is not a luxury, it is health, food security, and survival infrastructure.

1.2B people lack access to adequate cooling
420,000 deaths per year linked to broken cold chains
40% post-harvest food loss in Sub-Saharan Africa
The Opportunity

By achieving sustainable cooling we can:

Cut cooling emissions by 73% by 2050 through efficiency, refrigerant transition, and clean grids

UNEP Global Cooling Watch 2025

Relieve grid stress and reduce the frequency of power outages

Avoid the need for costly new power generation infrastructure

Prevent 0.5°C of warming this century by phasing down high-GWP refrigerants

UNEP, Kigali Amendment Overview

Save 1,300 TWh of electricity per year through global MEPS harmonization

UNSDSN CEET, Energy Rating Labels (2024)

Protect public health by drastically reducing heat-related illnesses and deaths

Secure food and medicine cold chains for the world's most vulnerable communities

SEforALL, Chilling Prospects 2025

The Framework

Five questions. Five pillars. One dashboard.

We organised CoolProgress around the questions policymakers actually ask, not the data we happened to have.

  • 01 How big is the cooling sector's emissions footprint? Emissions
  • 02 Are efficiency standards rising fast enough? Product Efficiency
  • 03 Is the HFC phase-down enough? Refrigerant Transition
  • 04 Who is being left behind on cooling access? Access & Vulnerability
  • 05 What is the state of pledges and national commitments? National Plans & Commitments

Each of the next five slides is one of these questions.

01 · Emissions

Country-level cooling emissions, direct and indirect, in one view.

Indirect emissions account for ~70% of the sector's footprint, driven by electricity use. Direct refrigerant leaks carry GWPs up to 2,088× CO₂. Both streams require distinct, concurrent policy responses.

  • Direct emissions: refrigerant leaks with GWPs up to 2,088× CO₂
  • Indirect emissions: approximately 70% of the sector's climate impact
  • Breakdown by appliance: air conditioners, refrigerators, fans

02 · Product Efficiency

Without binding standards, manufacturers dump their worst products in the fastest-growing markets.

The best AC on the market is 3× more efficient than the worst one still legally sold. Closing that gap globally could save 1,300 TWh of electricity per year — equivalent to shutting down hundreds of coal plants.

  • MEPS and energy label status for 80+ countries
  • Appliance coverage: air conditioners, refrigerators, ceiling fans
  • Policy adoption timeline, when standards were introduced and updated

03 · Refrigerant Transition

172 nations have ratified the Kigali Amendment. CoolProgress tracks whether they are following through.

Full Kigali compliance could prevent 0.5°C of warming this century. Yet the global refrigerant bank still holds an estimated 5–6 Mt of high-GWP gases circulating in active equipment today.

  • Ratification and implementation status for 195 countries
  • Article 5 Group 1, Group 2, and Non-Article 5 timelines tracked
  • Global refrigerant bank by compound, what's still circulating
Explore Kigali data →
04 · Access & Vulnerability

1.2 billion people face dangerous heat without adequate cooling. CoolProgress shows exactly where they are.

420,000 deaths per year are linked to broken cold chains. In Sub-Saharan Africa, 40% of post-harvest food is lost without adequate cold storage, disproportionately affecting the world's lowest-income households.

  • Population at risk by country, income group, urban and rural
  • Dimensions tracked: thermal safety, food security, cold chain access
  • Baseline for monitoring progress toward universal sustainable cooling
Explore access data →
05 · National Plans & Commitments

74 countries and 235 cities signed the Global Cooling Pledge at COP28.

The Pledge represents the largest ever political commitment to sustainable cooling. NDC cooling mentions and National Cooling Action Plans are tracked by CoolProgress, with data updates in progress.

  • Global Cooling Pledge signatory status for 195 countries
  • NDC cooling mentions analysed in partnership with Climate Policy Radar
  • National Cooling Action Plan status: completed, in progress, or absent
