# MEPS Review 2026 — Findings 2: Africa and Middle East (AC)

Research date: 2026-07-02. Patrick's flag confirmed: SADC, EAC, South Africa substantially wrong (too low); Nigeria structure right, absolute values unverifiable, label wrong; Saudi Arabia mixed units and cells from different tables; Ghana/ECOWAS wrong years/metrics.

## SADC Region — VERDICT: TOO LOW

- Standard: SADC HT 110:2023 "MEPS for Air Conditioners". Approved SADCSTAN ExCo 7 Nov 2023, circulated Feb 2024. Not self-executing; each member state must transpose. Only Mauritius enforces so far (MEPS Regulations 2025, enforced 1 Sep 2025, CSPF 4.5 for splits).
- Scope: non-ducted single-split, self-contained, portable ACs <= 16 kW. Metric: CSPF per ISO 16358-1:2013 (T1); portables EER (ISO 18326); reversible APF.
- MEPS (Table 10): <=4.5 kW: 4.50 (2024 Tier 1) -> 6.10 (2027+ Tier 2); 4.5-9.5 kW: 4.20 -> 5.10; 9.5-16 kW: 3.80 -> 4.50. Refrigerant caps: GWP <= 750 splits, <= 150 portables.
- CORRECTION: our 2024 "CSPF 3.2" -> 4.5; 2027 "3.8" -> 6.1 (<=4.5 kW class). Our 3.8 was Tier 1 of the 9.5-16 kW class (likely origin of error). Caveat: regional approval vs national transposition (only Mauritius enforcing).

## Nigeria — VERDICT: structure correct, absolute values UNVERIFIED, label wrong

- Current legal MEPS: NIS ECOSTAND 071-2:2017, EER 2.8 W/W. Poorly enforced (U4E market assessment: 19.6% of shop models below; market median EER 2.80-2.99).
- New MEPS: approved 25 Feb 2025 (not 2024), by Energy Commission of Nigeria (ECN) + SON with U4E/UNEP (Clean Cooling Collaborative). NOT "SON/NERC" (NERC = electricity regulator, uninvolved). Label: SON/ECN.
- Metric: NSEER (Nigeria-specific seasonal bins, ISO 16358-1 family). U4E quote: efficiency to improve "6% by 2026, 36% by 2029, and 48% by 2031" + refrigerant GWP cap from 2029.
- Our 3.18/4.08/4.44 = 3.0 x 1.06/1.36/1.48, i.e. assume baseline NSEER 3.0. Baseline 3.0 UNVERIFIED, no public source publishes absolute NSEER thresholds (SEER recommendations in unpublished report).
- CORRECTION: keep phased structure; relabel baseline year 2025, "SON/ECN MEPS (U4E supported)"; phases 2026 (+6%), 2029 (+36%), 2031 (+48%). Mark absolute values as estimated from assumed 3.0 baseline OR obtain SON standard text via Patrick/U4E.

## Saudi Arabia — VERDICT: units resolved, current pairings unsupportable

- SASO EER is in Btu/(W.h). SASO draft clause 3.36: "SEER: CSPF multiplied by 3.412" => CSPF = SASO SEER / 3.412. Our EER 6.8 -> CSPF 2.8 impossible (6.8/3.412 = 1.99).
- Current: SASO 2663:2021 (EER MEPS unchanged since 2018, in force 2022), Table 2, Btu/Wh: Window <=24k Btu/h: T1 9.80 / T3 7.00; Window 24-65k: 9.00/6.20; Split all <=65k: T1 11.80 / T3 8.30. No seasonal minimum today (SEER labelling-only).
- Our "2020 EER 6.8" matches no cell in any SASO version. Our "2025 EER 7.2" is the window <=24k T3 value of the 2025 revision, in force 23 Nov 2026 (date from jqccn.com only, UNVERIFIED elsewhere), not representative of splits.
- SASO 2663:2025 (draft Table 1): first-ever SEER minimums (Btu/Wh): window 8.50/7.80; split fixed-speed 10.10-10.30 (= CSPF 2.96-3.02); split variable-speed 12.80 (= CSPF 3.75). Split full-load EER stays 11.80 T1 / 8.40-8.50 T3.
- CORRECTION for CSPF chart: current regime footnoted "EER-only MEPS, T1 EER 11.8 Btu/Wh (3.46 W/W), no CSPF MEPS"; from Nov 2026: CSPF 2.96-3.02 (fixed splits) or 3.75 (inverter splits).

## Ghana and ECOWAS — VERDICT: wrong year/metric (Ghana); UNVERIFIED (ECOWAS 2026 point)

- Ghana: L.I. 2458 = Energy Efficiency Standards and Labelling (Air Conditioners) Regulations 2022 (not 2019), in force 2 Nov 2022, MEPS enforced 1 Dec 2025. Revoked L.I. 1815 (2005) which had EER 2.8 W/W. No 2019 AC MEPS event exists.
- L.I. 2458 (verified from Ghana's UNEP Ozone Secretariat submission): MEPS via AEER 3.66 W/W (non-ducted splits < 4 kW), 3.22 (4-10 kW); star rating via TSCPF per GS ISO 16358-1:2013, minimum TSCPF 2.60; scope to 65 kW.
- CORRECTION Ghana: 2005: EER 2.8 (L.I. 1815); 2022 (enforced Dec 2025): AEER 3.66 (<4 kW) + min TSCPF 2.60 (L.I. 2458). TSCPF 2.60 is the ISO-comparable floor; do NOT plot AEER as CSPF.
- ECOWAS: verified regional standard = ECOSTAND 071-2:2017 (adopted 2017, in force): minimum EER 2.8 W/W at 35C, scope <= 20 kW, ISO 5151/16358-1 referenced. Our "2026 proposed CSPF 3.2 ECREEE" UNVERIFIED and likely wrong (2026 belongs to Nigeria NSEER; "3.08" figures belong to ASEAN). Drop or replace with ECOSTAND 071-2:2017 (EER 2.8, 2017).

## Harmonization notes

- SADC HT 110 / EAS 1213 values are directly CSPF (ISO 16358-1, T1): no conversion. Plot <=4.5 kW class, state in tooltip.
- Ghana: TSCPF 2.60 is the ISO-comparable floor; AEER 3.66 is full-load type, not CSPF.
- Saudi: divide Btu/Wh by 3.412; only 2026 SEER minimums are seasonal.
- Nigeria NSEER: approx CSPF-comparable (Nigeria bins); flag baseline unverified.
- Distinguish regional approval vs national enforcement (SADC: only Mauritius; EAC: gazetted Jul 2025, transposition pending; Ghana: enforced Dec 2025).
